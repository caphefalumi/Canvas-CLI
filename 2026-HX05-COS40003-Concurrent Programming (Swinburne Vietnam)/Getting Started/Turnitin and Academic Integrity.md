# Turnitin and Academic Integrity

Assignments are typically submitted electronically through Canvas, and at Swinburne you will be expected to undertake and submit original work, whether in individual or group assignments. 

ELECTRONIC

Assessments are submitted electronically via Turnitin (available within Canvas). A condition of using Turnitin is that you have read and agree to the following statement:

I declare that in submitting all work for this assessment I have read, understood and agree to the content and expectations of the assessment declaration [http://www.swinburne.edu.au/student-administration/assessment/assessment-declaration.html]

TURNITIN

Turnitin is an important tool used in the learning community here at Swinburne. It can help you improve your writing as well as detecting plagiarism.

It's a commercial program which identifies the quality and originality of a written piece of work that you submit for assessment. It uses text matching software to detect similarity between your submission and previously presented work. The software uses a content database collected from millions of previously submitted works; from students, academic authors, publishing houses and so on. The software is currently used by many universities in Australia and internationally.

Your assignment or essay, when submitted through Canvas is usually submitted as a draft, and undergoes a comparison with the database. You'll receive an Similarity Report which will provide insights into where you can improve your writing and avoid plagiarism.

Staff and students can use Turnitin to review submissions for appropriateness of in-text citation, referencing, summarising and paraphrasing. Teaching staff at Swinburne use Turnitin to help identify plagiarism or inadequate academic writing practice.

INSTRUCTIONS ON HOW TO VIEW A TURNITIN SIMILARITY REPORT

A Similarity Report identifies the percentage of the assignment that is similar to other websites, books and journal articles. If a sentence or phrase is not cited correctly, for example, a direct quote is not in "quotations marks" and cited then this will be highlighted and made easily visible to the assessor. Naturally any material that is directly lifted from other sources will also be visible to the assessor.

When checking your Similarity Report, if you find large sections are highlighted, you might find that Turnitin has matched and highlighted direct quotes and references you have undertaken correctly in your bibliography. This may comprise a significant part of your correctly referenced assignment. You don't need to worry too much about this as the marker of your assignment will take this into consideration. You can find more information here on interpreting your Similarity Report. [https://guides.turnitin.com/01_Manuals_and_Guides/Instructor_Guides/Feedback_Studio/19_The_Similarity_Report/Viewing_the_Similarity_Report]

Unfortunately Turnitin has become increasingly slow. The initial Similarity Report can take up to half an hour to be generated. For any subsequent & resubmitted assignments it can take up to 24 hours to generate this Similarity Report. This means if you'd like to check your whether your assignment is correctly referenced it can be good idea to submit a draft 24 hours before the due date and make any adjustments (should these be needed) prior to the final submission. 

The primary purpose of Turnitin is to enable us to mark your assignments electronically.
